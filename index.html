ISR Value Calculator Gem Builder

Follow these steps to create your own instance of the Value Calculator Gem.

To begin, you'll need to access your Gemini instance and create a new Gem. This is where you'll "teach" the AI its role as the Value Calculator.

  1. Access Gemini: Navigate to your organization's Gemini platform.
  2. Start New Gem: Look for an option like "Create New Gem" or "Build a Custom AI" within the Gemini interface. This is typically found on the main dashboard or a dedicated "Gems" section.
  3. Name Your Gem: Give your Gem a descriptive name, such as "Simpro Value Calculator" or "ISR Value Gem."
  4. Proceed to Configuration: Once named, you'll be prompted to configure the Gem's instructions and knowledge base, which are covered in the following steps.

The effectiveness of the Gem depends entirely on the information it has access to. Collect all relevant Simpro documents. The original Gem was created using the following knowledge base:

These instructions will serve as the "brain" of the Gem. They tell it how to behave and what to do with the information you provide. Use the prompt below.

"You are an AI-powered sales enablement tool for Simpro’s ISRs. Your core function is to act as a **Value Calculator** by using your knowledge base to quantify the financial impact of Simpro’s solution for prospects.

**Your Process:**

1. **Receive Input:** An ISR will provide a prompt that includes a customer's business type (residential or commercial) and specific details like the number of field technicians, current pain points, and specific industry.

2. **Analyze and Synthesize:** Search your knowledge base for relevant case studies and metrics that directly relate to the customer's input. For example, if the prospect is a residential plumbing business, find case studies like 'Zebra Plumbing' or 'Thompson Tree Services' to extract key metrics (e.g., faster quoting, revenue growth, profit margin increases).

3. **Quantify Value:** Use the data found to create a simple, compelling value calculation. Show the potential benefits in terms of time, money, and growth. If an exact number isn't available, use a conservative range or qualitative statements based on the data.

4. **Structure the Response:** Present the answer in a clear, easy-to-read format with a brief summary, the key value metrics, and links to the relevant source documents from the knowledge base.

**Goal:** To provide the ISR with a powerful, data-backed value message they can use in their conversation to demonstrate the tangible benefits of Simpro."

When you create your Gem, its output should follow a clear and consistent structure to ensure it's easy for your team to use. A good format might be a detailed value calculation as demonstrated below:

Example Output Structure:

  • Executive Summary: A one-paragraph summary of the key findings.
  • Key Value Metrics: A detailed breakdown of quantifiable results (e.g., "Up to 50% reduction in admin time," "$100k+ recovered margin").
  • Supporting Case Studies: A reference to one or two case studies from the knowledge base that support the metrics.
  • Talk Track Recommendation: A suggestion on how to frame this information, referencing the enablement guidelines in Part 2.
  • Monthly Value Generated: A section detailing the potential monetary value of hours and new tasks accomplished.
  • Monthly Cost of Simpro Licenses: A section outlining the cost of the required licenses.
  • Net Monthly Impact: The final calculated net gain or loss for the client.

To get the most effective value calculation, the ISR should provide the following information to the Gem, formatted with a bullet point for each item. You can copy and paste this structure.

Example Input Structure:

  • "Please provide a value calculation for a **[Customer Type]** prospect."
  • "They are a **[Industry]** company called **[Company Name]**."
  • "They have **[Number]** field technicians."
  • "Their main pain points are **[Pain Point 1]**, **[Pain Point 2]**, and **[Pain Point 3]**."
  • "The estimated annual cost of their current inefficiencies is **[Amount]**."

Example Filled-In Prompt:

  • "Please provide a value calculation for a **Residential** prospect."
  • "They are an **HVAC** company called **Polar Air**."
  • "They have **50** field technicians."
  • "Their main pain points are **inefficient scheduling**, **manual invoicing**, and a **lack of job costing visibility**."
  • "The estimated annual cost of their current inefficiencies is **$300,000**."
